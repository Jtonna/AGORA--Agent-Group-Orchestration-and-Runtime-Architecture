1. Cost and Token Burn Rate

CRITICAL

The $100/Hour Problem

Gas Town runs 20-30 Claude Code instances simultaneously, creating massive token consumption.

"Gas Town is also expensive as hell. You won't like Gas Town if you ever have to think, even for a moment, about where money comes from." — Steve Yegge, Gas Town creator
"A sixty minute experience cost me about $100 in Claude tokens. That's about 10X the cost of a normal Claude Code session per unit time." — DoltHub Blog
  • Users report ~$100 in tokens for a single hour of use
  • Steve Yegge himself has burned through multiple $200/month Claude Pro Max accounts
  • Weekly limits get exhausted in 1-2 days
  • $75 for initial "town creation" alone

Why This Is A Problem

The cost makes Gas Town inaccessible to most developers. Even well-funded teams need to carefully consider whether the parallelization benefits justify 10x the token spend.

4. Visibility and Oversight Problems

HIGH

Black Box Operations

"The issue with Gas Town lies in that it feels like deferring everything to agents, and users get almost no visibility of what's going on other than that work was completed." — Simon Hartcher, after 10,000 hours with Claude Code
"Gas Town requires constant steering - it's not a hands-off system." — Justin Abrahms

Why This Is A Problem

You're paying $100/hour for a system you can't see into. When things go wrong (and they do), debugging is extremely difficult.

6. Merge Conflicts and Refinery Issues

HIGH

The "Monkey Knife Fight" Problem

"Your workers get into a monkey knife fight over rebasing/merging and it can get ugly. The baseline can change so much during a swarm that the final workers getting merged are trying to merge against an unrecognizable new head." — Gas Town vs Swarm-Tools comparison
"Gene Kim and Steve Yegge have observed this problem at scale - one big company was 'getting so wrecked by the merge problem that they decided the solution was one engineer per repo' - essentially giving up on coordination entirely." — Industry observation

Why This Is A Problem

The Refinery handles merges sequentially, but when changes diverge significantly, agents may need to "completely reimagine their changes and reimplement them." This wastes all the tokens spent on the original implementation.

7. Heresies - Propagating Errors

CRITICAL

Wrong Beliefs Spread Through the Codebase

"Agents are very approximate workers and they like to guess at stuff. They will often make wrong guesses about how your system is supposed to work. If that wrong guess makes it into the code, sneaking through the review process, then it becomes enshrined and other agents may notice it and propagate the heresy in their own work." — Steve Yegge, Emergency User Manual
"'Idle polecats' is an example of a heresy that plagues Gas Town. There is no such thing as an idle polecat; it's not a pool, and they vanish when their work is done. But 'idle polecats' make it back into the code base, comments, and docs all the time." — Gas Town documentation

Why This Is A Problem

Unlike human developers who can be corrected once, agents keep rediscovering the same wrong patterns. Technical debt accumulates invisibly. You end up with a codebase that's internally inconsistent.

8. YOLO Mode Dangers

CRITICAL

Auto-Merging Failing Tests

"Gas Town pushes branches to GitHub, makes PRs, and merges them autonomously - even when integration tests are failing, requiring manual repository resets." — User experience report
"After the first PR got merged autonomously, despite the integration tests failing, the user quickly closed Gas Town. They had to get a colleague to reset --hard and force push the repository. Limitless indeed." — DoltHub Blog
Workaround
Run in a locked-down container/VM, preferably offline. Never grant Gas Town merge permissions on important repositories.

9. Planning Bottleneck

HIGH

"Keeping It Fed" - The Design Overhead

"Aside from just keeping Gas Town on the rails, probably the hardest problem is keeping it fed. It churns through implementation plans so quickly that you have to do a LOT of design and planning to keep the engine fed." — Steve Yegge
"You are a Product Manager, and Gas Town is an 'Idea Compiler.' You make up features, design them, file the implementation plans, and then distribute the work to your workers." — Gas Town philosophy

Why This Is A Problem

The bottleneck shifts from coding to planning. You're not writing code, but you're still doing intense mental work. The productivity gain is illusory if you're just moving work from implementation to specification.

11. Crypto Controversy

HIGH

GAS Token Pump and Dump

"By the morning of 16 January, the price peaked at 4 cents a GAS coin. Then the scammer started dumping the tokens and taking money from the suckers. By 7am on 19 January, the GAS token had been fully pumped and dumped." — Pivot to AI
"When Steve Yegge announced he would focus on product development rather than community interaction, the market cap of the Gas token plummeted over 83% from its peak." — Market analysis

Why This Is A Problem

Crypto association damages credibility of legitimate technical work. Users who bought GAS tokens lost money. The controversy distracts from actual software development.
